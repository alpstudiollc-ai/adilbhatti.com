Frequently Asked Questions

Everything readers ask.

What is the Salah, Shawarma, Success!! Notebook?
It is a high-energy Islamic daily-wins journal by author Adil Bhatti, designed for achievement-driven Muslim men who track their five daily prayers, their meals, and their wins with equal enthusiasm. It features 200 college-ruled lined pages with Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Raheem in Arabic calligraphy on every page, and a triple-pop cover that nails the Muslim brother mood. Available in hardcover (ASIN B0FK5797HN) and paperback (ASIN B0FJR64XSG) on Amazon.
Is this a good gift for a Muslim man focused on daily wins or self-improvement?
Yes, this notebook was made for exactly that brother. For the Muslim man tracking habits, chasing daily goals, lifting weights, launching a business, or just trying to string together three good days in a row — this notebook holds the scoreboard. Each Bismillah-headed page keeps the chase grounded in niyyah.
Can this notebook be used as a five daily prayers tracker?
Yes. Many Muslim brothers use the Salah, Shawarma, Success!! Notebook to log their five daily prayers (Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha), note whether each was on time and in congregation, and track sunnah prayers, witr, and tahajjud across the week. The open college-ruled pages let each brother build his own tracking style.
Is it suitable for Muslim goal setting and habit tracking?
Absolutely. Goal setting, habit stacking, quarterly review, monthly OKRs, weekly wins, daily scoreboards — this notebook handles every flavor of intentional living. Muslim men use it for fitness goals, Quran reading targets, business milestones, relationship goals, dhikr counts, and more.
Does every page have Bismillah in Arabic?
Yes. All 200 college-ruled lined pages begin with بِسْمِ ٱللَّٰهِ ٱلرَّحْمَـٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ (Bismillah ar-Rahman ar-Raheem) in elegant Arabic calligraphy. Success without Bismillah is just noise — every entry in this notebook starts in the name of Allah.
Is it a good Ramadan gift for a Muslim brother?
Yes. Ramadan is where Muslim men tend to reset goals, re-stack habits, and recalibrate priorities. This notebook is ideal for logging daily Ramadan wins — Taraweeh attendance, Quran juz completed, fasts kept strong, sadaqah given, tahajjud made, iftars shared. The hardcover is a standout Ramadan gift for the achievement-minded brother.

Yes. The hardcover edition is a memorable Eid al-Fitr or Eid al-Adha gift for a Muslim man who tracks his wins and his deen closely. Pairs well with cologne, a halal food gift basket, or a premium prayer mat.
