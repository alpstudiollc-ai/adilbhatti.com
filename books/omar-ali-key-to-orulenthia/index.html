Omar & Ali: The Key to Orulenthia

Two brothers. One ancient relic. A red-skied world that never should have existed — but did, and now does, and now knows they're there.

A 243-page young adult science fiction fantasy novel by author Adil Bhatti. On a family trip to Tangier, Morocco, American brothers Omar and Ali pick up an artifact older than any museum could date — and it wakes up. What comes next bends space, folds time, and drops them onto Orulenthia: an alien world under a burning red sun, with its own rules, its own dangers, and a reason they were the ones who arrived. Cross-dimensional portal fantasy for fans of Percy Jackson, A Wrinkle in Time, Artemis Fowl, and anyone who's ever looked at a strange object and wondered what it was waiting for.

The Story

A relic wakes up. A doorway opens. Two brothers are the ones holding it.

It starts the way the best adventures always do — with something that was supposed to be ordinary and turns out not to be.

Omar and Ali are American brothers on a family trip to Tangier, Morocco. One ancient relic, one wrong (or very right) touch, and a line they didn't know existed between this world and another is suddenly wide open. The stars rearrange. The ground pulls sideways. And when they can breathe again, they're standing under a sun the color of fire, in a land called Orulenthia — a world with a red sky, a horizon they don't recognize, and something watching that knows exactly who they are.

Home is on the other side of something they don't know how to close.

What follows is a 243-page YA sci-fi fantasy adventure about two brothers who have to figure out, in real time, what the relic actually unlocked — and whether they're here by accident, by design, or because Orulenthia was waiting. It's a portal fantasy built for readers who like their alien worlds vivid, their sibling dynamics real, and their stakes higher than the sky above them.

For the YA reader who's been chasing the feeling of a first read of Percy Jackson or A Wrinkle in Time. For the teen who wants an adventure where the heroes happen to look a little more like the world they live in. For anyone who's ever picked up an old thing in a foreign place and wondered what it would do if they held it for just a second longer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything readers ask.

What is Omar & Ali: The Key to Orulenthia about?
It is a young adult science fiction fantasy novel by Adil Bhatti. Two American brothers, Omar and Ali, activate an ancient relic during a family trip to Tangier, Morocco, and are pulled across space-time to a red-skied alien world called Orulenthia. It is a 243-page portal fantasy adventure for YA readers and sci-fi fans.
What genre is this book?
The book is a young adult science fiction fantasy novel, specifically a portal fantasy with cross-dimensional sci-fi adventure elements. It blends YA sci-fi, YA fantasy, coming-of-age adventure, and alien-world worldbuilding. Readers who enjoyed Percy Jackson, A Wrinkle in Time, Artemis Fowl, or His Dark Materials will likely feel at home.
Is this book suitable for teens?
Yes. Omar & Ali: The Key to Orulenthia is written for young adult readers ages 12 and up, with appeal reaching from upper middle-grade readers through high school. It is appropriate for classroom libraries, school reading lists, family reading, and teen book clubs.
Is this an Islamic book?
No. Omar & Ali: The Key to Orulenthia is a secular young adult science fiction fantasy novel. Omar and Ali are American brothers who happen to be Muslim, which is one detail of their identity, but the book is a sci-fi portal fantasy adventure — not religious fiction, not Islamic didactic literature, and not aimed exclusively at Muslim readers. It is for anyone who loves YA sci-fi fantasy.
Does the book feature diverse characters?
Yes. The two teenage protagonists, Omar and Ali, are American brothers with Muslim backgrounds — representation that is still underrepresented in mainstream YA sci-fi fantasy. Readers searching for diverse YA, multicultural fantasy, own-voices science fiction, or YA novels with Muslim heroes will find this book meaningful, while sci-fi and fantasy readers of all backgrounds will find a genre adventure they can enjoy without prerequisite.
Where is the book set?
The novel opens in the real-world setting of Tangier, Morocco, where the brothers activate an ancient relic that pulls them across space-time. Most of the adventure takes place in Orulenthia, a fictional alien world with a red sky and its own landscape, inhabitants, and rules of magic and technology.
