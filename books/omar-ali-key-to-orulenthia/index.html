The Story

Two brothers. One talisman. A world full of reasons it was hidden.

It starts the way most adventures don't — with something dusty, something forgotten, something nobody was supposed to hand to a curious teenager on vacation.

Omar and Ali are ordinary Muslim American teen brothers from New Jersey. Video games, school, family dinners, the usual. Then comes the family trip to Morocco, and in the alleys of an old market, they stumble onto a mysterious talisman that nobody can quite explain. The brothers bring it home. The talisman waits. And then, on one unremarkable afternoon in the suburbs of New Jersey, it wakes up.

One accidental activation. Space folds. Stars rearrange. Home disappears.

What the talisman fires open is a portal — and what's on the other side is Orulenthia. A planet under a massive red giant sun. Floating mountains drifting over an alien landscape. Telepathic beings who already knew they were coming. Ancient alien temples pulsing with energy the brothers don't yet understand. And a brutal tyrant who has ruled the world with an iron fist for longer than anyone dares to remember.

The brothers don't land as tourists. They land as problems — and opportunities. Before they've figured out which way is up on Orulenthia, they're swept into a secret rebellion led by Vaan, an enigmatic warrior-scholar with his own reasons for wanting to see the tyrant fall. Glider chases tear across crimson skies. Ancient temples reveal what the talisman actually is. And every step deeper into the rebellion tests the brothers on things no teenager in New Jersey was supposed to face — their courage, their faith, their bond.

A fast-paced YA sci-fi fantasy adventure built for readers who want worldbuilding with real stakes, sibling dynamics that feel honest, and a portal fantasy that earns its alien world. Crafted as halal entertainment: clean, values-based, wholesome for Muslim households and any parent looking for storytelling they can hand over without hesitation — and still a legitimate genre adventure that stands up to the shelf it's on.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything readers ask.

What is Omar & Ali: The Key to Orulenthia about?
It is a young adult science fiction fantasy novel by Adil Bhatti. Two Muslim American teen brothers from New Jersey stumble upon a mysterious ancient talisman during a family trip to Morocco. Back home in the United States, the talisman accidentally fires open a portal to Orulenthia — an alien world under a massive red giant sun, with floating mountains, telepathic beings, and a brutal tyrant ruling with an iron fist. The brothers join a secret rebellion led by the enigmatic warrior-scholar Vaan. It is a 243-page fast-paced portal fantasy adventure for YA readers and sci-fi fans.
What genre is this book?
The book is a young adult science fiction fantasy novel — specifically a portal fantasy with cross-dimensional sci-fi adventure, space opera worldbuilding, and coming-of-age themes. It sits comfortably alongside Percy Jackson, Stargate, A Wrinkle in Time, Star Trek, Dune, Ender's Game, and Star Wars as a comparable reading experience. Clean, values-based storytelling throughout.
Where does the story take place?
The story begins in New Jersey, USA, where Omar and Ali live as ordinary teen brothers. It briefly visits Tangier, Morocco during a family vacation — the location where they stumble upon the ancient talisman. The talisman is then brought home to New Jersey, where it accidentally fires open the portal. The majority of the adventure unfolds on Orulenthia — a fictional alien world with red skies under a massive red giant sun, floating mountains, telepathic inhabitants, and a brutal tyrant's regime the brothers find themselves caught in.
Who are Omar and Ali?
Omar and Ali are two curious Muslim American teenage brothers from New Jersey. They are the novel's protagonists — ordinary teens from an ordinary American suburb whose accidental discovery of a mysterious talisman in Morocco, and its later activation at home, pulls them into an extraordinary intergalactic rebellion. Their sibling bond and the way it's tested across the book is central to the story.
Who is Vaan?
Vaan is the enigmatic warrior-scholar who leads the secret rebellion against the tyrant ruling Orulenthia. He becomes a pivotal ally to Omar and Ali once they arrive in the alien world, alongside a cast of memorable rebellion fighters the brothers meet along the way.
What is Orulenthia like?
Orulenthia is an alien world like nothing the brothers have ever seen. Its skies glow red under the light of a massive red giant sun. Floating mountains drift above its landscape. Telepathic beings call it home. Ancient alien temples pulse with strange energy. And a brutal tyrant rules the planet with an iron fist, oppressing its inhabitants — until a secret rebellion, now joined by Omar and Ali, decides to fight back.

No. Omar & Ali: The Key to Orulenthia is a young adult science fiction fantasy novel — not religious didactic literature. What is "halal entertainment"?
Halal entertainment refers to fiction, film, and media that delivers compelling storytelling while staying free of content that conflicts with Islamic values — no gratuitous sexuality, no profanity, no substance-glorifying content, no material that compromises core principles. Omar & Ali: The Key to Orulenthia is a rare piece of halal entertainment built at genre quality — a legitimate YA sci-fi fantasy novel that Muslim families and any parent seeking clean reading can confidently hand to a teen.
What is the ancient talisman in the story?
The talisman is a mysterious ancient artifact the brothers stumble upon during their family vacation in Morocco. Unbeknownst to them, it is a key — a dormant device capable of firing open a portal to another world. When they bring it home to New Jersey, it eventually activates, and the adventure begins. Its origin, its purpose, and its connection to Orulenthia are some of the central mysteries the story unfolds.
