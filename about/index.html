About the Author

Meet Adil Bhatti.

American-Pakistani author. Traveler. Foodie. Sci-fi devotee. Electric guitar picker.

Adil Bhatti writes stories that cross continents, cultures, and dimensions. His catalog includes young adult science fiction, investigative non-fiction on UFOs and ancient mysteries, and a line of ten bold Islamic notebooks. All works are published under his independent imprint, Alpha Leonis Production Studio LLC, and available on Amazon worldwide.

The Story

The writer behind the books.

Adil Bhatti is an American-Pakistani author whose work crosses continents, cultures, and dimensions. With his home in the United States, he has lived, traveled, and eaten his way through Türkiye, Morocco, Dubai, Japan, England, the Netherlands, France, Belgium, Spain, China, Hong Kong, Taiwan, and far beyond, and that restless curiosity shows up in every line he writes. Sci-fi shaped his taste early, space has stayed on his mind ever since, and the older the question, the more likely it is to end up in one of his books.

His catalog ranges from young adult sci-fi adventure, to investigative non-fiction on UFOs and ancient mysteries, to boldly designed notebooks for readers who want their stationery to say something. At the center of all of it: family, and food worth traveling for.

"Stories travel. The best ones honor both imagination and specificity."

That range is not an accident. Living across multiple continents teaches a writer that characters who feel most universal are usually the most specific, that wonder does not need permission, and that a good portal fantasy and a good UFO investigation are asking the exact same question from opposite ends: what is out there, and what does it mean that we keep noticing? When Adil isn't writing, he is usually chasing that same question sideways — jamming on an electric guitar, disappearing into a sprawling video game world, or letting a film take him somewhere new. Music, gaming, and cinema are not distractions from the writing; they are feedback loops for it.

Reading is its own lane. Adil reads widely across science fiction, history, and investigative non-fiction, and those reading habits show up directly in the work. His young adult novel Omar & Ali: The Key to Orulenthia follows two Muslim American teenage brothers who activate an ancient relic in Tangier, Morocco, and are pulled across space-time to a red-skied alien world. His non-fiction book Ancient Aliens to Modern UFOs: The Disclosure Revolution walks the reader from biblical UFO encounters and the 1561 Nuremberg event through to Congressional UAP disclosure hearings and the USS Nimitz Tic Tac incident. His ten Islamic notebooks carry Bismillah calligraphy on every one of their 200 college-ruled pages, designed for a new generation of Muslim readers who want their stationery to reflect their identity.

All of it is published under Alpha Leonis Production Studio LLC, his independent imprint. All of it is available on Amazon in paperback, hardcover, and where applicable Kindle. The books are additionally distributed through IngramSpark to retailers including Barnes & Noble, Walmart, Powell's, Strand Book Store, Booktopia, AbeBooks, and independent bookstores worldwide. Writing is the main lane. Travel, family, food, music, guitar, games, and movies are the rest stops that keep it interesting.

Author FAQ

People ask. Adil answers.

Who is Adil Bhatti the author?
Adil Bhatti is an American-Pakistani author based in the United States. He writes young adult science fiction, investigative non-fiction on UFOs and ancient mysteries, and publishes a line of bold Islamic notebooks. He publishes independently under Alpha Leonis Production Studio LLC.
What is Adil Bhatti's background?
Adil Bhatti is an American-Pakistani writer whose home is in the United States. He has lived and traveled across Türkiye, Morocco, Dubai, Japan, England, and beyond, and those cross-cultural experiences directly shape the characters, settings, and questions in his books.
What nationality is Adil Bhatti?
Adil Bhatti is American-Pakistani. He holds the American and Pakistani heritage equally and writes from both identities, with his primary residence in the United States.
What countries has Adil Bhatti lived in or visited?
Adil Bhatti has lived, traveled, and eaten his way through the United States, Pakistan, Türkiye, Morocco, Dubai (UAE), Japan, England, the Netherlands, France, Belgium, Spain, China, Hong Kong, Taiwan, and many more countries beyond that list. Travel is a core part of his identity as both a person and a writer.
What genres does Adil Bhatti write in?
Adil Bhatti writes across three distinct categories: young adult science fiction and fantasy (Omar & Ali: The Key to Orulenthia), investigative non-fiction on UFO disclosure and ancient mysteries (Ancient Aliens to Modern UFOs: The Disclosure Revolution), and a line of ten bold Islamic notebooks with Bismillah calligraphy on every page.
Why does Adil Bhatti write about UFOs?
The UFO work is where his earliest instincts meet his research discipline. Sci-fi shaped his taste, space has always pulled his attention, and ancient mysteries tie it all together. His non-fiction treatment of UFO disclosure is grounded in declassified Pentagon files, Congressional UAP hearings, and verified historical records, written for readers who want evidence, not hype.
Who publishes Adil Bhatti's books?
All of Adil Bhatti's books and notebooks are published under his independent imprint, Alpha Leonis Production Studio LLC. The imprint handles editorial, cover design, print distribution via Amazon KDP and IngramSpark, and digital distribution via Kindle.
Is Adil Bhatti Muslim?
Adil Bhatti writes from a Muslim perspective and his catalog includes explicitly Muslim works such as Omar & Ali: The Key to Orulenthia (a young adult fantasy with Muslim heroes) and a series of ten Islamic notebooks featuring Bismillah calligraphy on every page. His UFO non-fiction, however, is a secular investigation of the UAP disclosure movement and is written for readers of all backgrounds.
Where can I follow Adil Bhatti online?
Adil Bhatti is active on Instagram and Threads at @6MillionRupeeMan. Readers can also follow him on his Amazon author page for new release notifications. Direct email: adil@adilbhatti.com.
Does Adil Bhatti accept media, interview, or partnership requests?
Yes. Adil Bhatti can be reached directly at adil@adilbhatti.com for press, podcast, interview, review copy requests, retailer inquiries, partnerships, and reader questions.
What are Adil Bhatti's main interests outside of writing?
Outside of writing, Adil Bhatti is a lifelong lover of science fiction and space, an avid reader, a dedicated traveler, and a passionate foodie. He jams on the electric guitar, listens to music constantly, sinks hours into video games, and considers a good film as productive as a good reading session. Family is the constant at the center of everything he does.
What inspired Adil Bhatti to become a writer?
A combination of global experience, family, and an unshakeable curiosity about the universe. Living across multiple continents and cultures taught Adil Bhatti that stories travel, and that the best ones honor both imagination and specificity. His work reflects the places he has been and the wonder he has never let go of.
